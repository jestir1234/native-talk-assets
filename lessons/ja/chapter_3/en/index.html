Chapter 3: Basic Greetings & Introductions

Let's unlock the secrets of polite Japanese interactions!

🌟 Welcome to Your First Japanese Conversations!

Ready to start speaking Japanese? In this chapter, you'll learn essential greetings and how to introduce yourself. Mastering these phrases is your first step to making Japanese friends!

🗣️ Greetings - Say Hello Any Time!

Japanese greetings change depending on the time of day. Let's learn the most common ones!

こんにちは

Konnichiwa

Hello / Good afternoon

✨ Used widely from late morning to early evening. It's your all-purpose "Hello"!

おはよう

Ohayou

Good morning (informal)

⏰ Used in the morning. If you want to be more polite (e.g., to a teacher or boss), add "gozaimasu": Ohayou gozaimasu (おはようございます).

こんばんは

Konbanwa

Good evening

🌙 Used from evening until night. Sounds similar to Konnichiwa, but has a different meaning!

さようなら

Sayounara

Goodbye

👋 This is a formal "Goodbye" for when you won't see someone for a long time. For daily goodbyes with friends/family, Japanese people often use "mata ne" (またね - See you) or just "jaa ne" (じゃあね - Bye!).

🙋‍♀️ Introducing Yourself - "My Name Is..."

Ready to tell people your name? Here's the most basic way to introduce yourself.

わたしは 〇〇です。

Watashi wa 〇〇 desu.

I am 〇〇.

Simply replace 〇〇 with your name!

Examples:

  • わたしは マイク です。
  • わたしは エマ です。
  • わたしは ケン です。

Grammar Spotlight: Particle は (wa) & Copula です (desu)

You just used two super important grammar points:

  • は (wa): The Topic Particle

    This little particle marks the topic of the sentence. Even though it's written 'ha', it's pronounced 'wa' when used as a particle! Think of it like saying "As for me..." or "Speaking of me...".

    Example: わたしは (watashi wa) - "As for me..."

  • です (desu): The Copula "To Be"

    This is the polite form of "is," "am," or "are." It comes at the end of a sentence and makes your sentence polite. Without it, your sentence would sound very blunt or informal.

    Example: 〇〇です (〇〇 desu) - "is 〇〇" / "am 〇〇"

Meeting Someone for the First Time:

はじめまして。

Hajimemashite.

Nice to meet you. (First time meeting)

🎉 This literally means "it's the first time" and is exclusively used when meeting someone for the very first time. Always say this!

よろしくおねがいします。

Yoroshiku Onegaishimasu.

Please treat me well. / I look forward to working with you. / Nice to meet you. (Formal closing)

🙏 This phrase is hard to translate directly, but it's a very common and polite way to close an introduction. It conveys a sense of asking for a good relationship or cooperation. Always use it after "Hajimemashite"!

Cultural Note: Politeness in Japanese 🙇

Japanese society places a high value on politeness and respect, especially towards elders, superiors, or people you are meeting for the first time. The grammar we've learned (`です`) is a basic step in polite language.

When you introduce yourself, the combination of Hajimemashite and Yoroshiku Onegaishimasu shows you are respectful and keen on establishing a good relationship. It's not just about saying your name; it's about showing respect and humility from the start.
