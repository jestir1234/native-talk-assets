# Japanese Chapter 7 Quiz: Essential Verbs & Conjugation Basics

## Question 1
type: multipleChoice
question: What does いく mean?
options:
  - To go
  - To come
  - To eat
  - To do
correctAnswer: To go
explanation: いく (iku) means "to go" in Japanese.

## Question 6
type: dragAndDrop
question: Form the Japanese sentence "I eat bread"
draggableItems:
  - 私
  - は
  - パン
  - を
  - 食べます
correctAnswer: 私はパンを食べます
explanation: This is the polite form of "to eat" using 食べます (tabemasu).

## Question 7
type: multipleChoice
question: What does パンをたべません mean?
options:
  - I eat bread
  - I don't eat bread
  - I will eat bread
  - I ate bread
correctAnswer: I don't eat bread
explanation: パンをたべません (pan wo tabemasen) is the negative form meaning "I don't eat bread."

## Question 8
type: multipleChoice
question: What is the polite form of いく (to go)?
options:
  - いきます
  - いきません

  - いかない
correctAnswer: いきます
explanation: いきます (ikimasu) is the polite present/future form of いく (iku).
