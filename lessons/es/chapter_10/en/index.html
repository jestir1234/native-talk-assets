Chapter 10: Time and Schedules

🕒 Telling Time: ¿Qué hora es?

Learning to tell time is super practical! In Spanish, we mostly use the 12-hour clock, but the 24-hour format is also common, especially in official settings or when talking about schedules.

Asking the Time

  • ¿Qué hora es? [keh OH-rah es] What time is it?

Answering the Time

For 1:00, we use singular "Es la...":

  • Es la una. [es lah OO-nah] It's one o'clock.

For all other hours, we use plural "Son las...":

  • Son las dos. [sohn lahs dohs] It's two o'clock.
  • Son las diez. [sohn lahs dyehs] It's ten o'clock.

Minutes: Adding Detail

After the hour, you add "y" (and) followed by the minutes. For example:

  • Son las tres y cinco. [sohn lahs trehs ee SEEN-koh] It's 3:05.
  • Son las ocho y veinticinco. [sohn lahs OH-choh ee veyn-tee-SEEN-koh] It's 8:25.

Special phrases for common times:

  • y cuarto [ee KWAHR-toh] a quarter past (15 minutes)
  • y media [ee MEH-dya] half past (30 minutes)
  • menos cuarto [MEH-nohs KWAHR-toh] a quarter to (15 minutes before the next hour)

Son las siete y cuarto.

It's 7:15.

Son las once y media.

It's 11:30.

Son las ocho menos cuarto.

It's a quarter to eight (7:45).

Morning, Afternoon, Evening

  • de la mañana [deh lah mah-NYAH-nah] in the morning (AM)
  • de la tarde [deh lah TAHR-deh] in the afternoon (PM, generally until sunset)
  • de la noche [deh lah NOH-cheh] in the evening/night (PM, from sunset onwards)

Es la una de la tarde.

It's 1:00 PM.

Son las siete de la noche.

It's 7:00 PM.

Special Times

  • Mediodía [meh-dyoh-DEE-ah] Noon
  • Medianoche [meh-dyah-NOH-cheh] Midnight

🗓️ Days of the Week

In Spanish, the days of the week are not capitalized unless they start a sentence. They are also masculine, typically used with "el" (the) for a specific day, or "los" (the plural) for "on Mondays," "every Monday," etc.

lunes [LOO-nehs] Monday
martes [MAHR-tehs] Tuesday
miércoles [MYEHR-koh-lehs] Wednesday
jueves [HWEH-vehs] Thursday
viernes [VYEHR-nehs] Friday
sábado [SAH-bah-doh] Saturday
domingo [doh-MEEN-goh] Sunday

Using Days in Sentences

  • Hoy es lunes. [oy es LOO-nehs] Today is Monday.
  • Mañana es martes. [mah-NYAH-nah es MAHR-tehs] Tomorrow is Tuesday.
  • Ayer fue domingo. [ah-YEHR fweh doh-MEEN-goh] Yesterday was Sunday.

El jueves tengo una reunión.

On Thursday I have a meeting.

Los domingos vamos a la playa.

On Sundays we go to the beach (or "Every Sunday we go to the beach").

📅 Months of the Year

Similar to days of the week, months in Spanish are not capitalized unless they start a sentence.

enero [eh-NEH-roh] January
febrero [feh-BREH-roh] February
marzo [MAHR-soh] March
abril [ah-BREEL] April
mayo [MAH-yoh] May
junio [HOO-nyoh] June
julio [HOO-lyoh] July
agosto [ah-GOHS-toh] August
septiembre [sehp-TYEHM-breh] September
octubre [ohk-TOO-breh] October
noviembre [noh-VYEHM-breh] November
diciembre [dee-SYEHM-breh] December

To say "in [month]", use "en":

Mi cumpleaños es en marzo.

My birthday is in March.

Viajamos en julio.

We travel in July.

🗓️ When is...? It's on...

To ask "When is something?", you use "¿Cuándo es...?"

  • ¿Cuándo es tu cumpleaños? [KWAHN-doh es too koom-pleh-AH-nyohs] When is your birthday?

To state a date, you usually say "It's the [number] of [month]". Use "el" before the number.

Es el + [number] + de + [month]

¿Cuándo es la fiesta?

When is the party?

Es el veinte de mayo.

It's on May 20th.

Mi cumpleaños es el siete de abril.

My birthday is on April 7th.

La clase es el lunes, dos de octubre.

The class is on Monday, October 2nd.

Cultural Note: In Spanish-speaking countries, dates are often written day/month/year (e.g., 20/05/2024 for May 20, 2024).

🤝 Making Appointments and Schedules

Here are some useful phrases for discussing plans and availability.

Key Vocabulary

  • una cita [OO-nah SEE-tah] an appointment / a date
  • tener una cita [teh-NEHR OO-nah SEE-tah] to have an appointment
  • ¿Estás libre? (informal) [es-TAHS LEE-breh] Are you free?
  • ¿Está libre? (formal) [es-TAH LEE-breh] Are you free?
  • Estoy libre. [es-TOY LEE-breh] I'm free.
  • Estoy ocupado/a. [es-TOY oh-koo-PAH-doh/dah] I'm busy (m/f).

Suggesting & Confirming

  • ¿Te viene bien el martes? [teh VYEH-neh byehn el MAHR-tehs] Does Tuesday work for you?
  • ¿A qué hora? [ah keh OH-rah] At what time?
  • A las tres. [ah lahs trehs] At three o'clock.
  • De acuerdo. [deh ah-KWEHR-doh] Agreed / Okay.

Quiero hacer una cita con el médico.

I want to make an appointment with the doctor.

¿Estás libre el viernes por la tarde?

Are you free on Friday afternoon?

Sí, estoy libre. ¿A qué hora?

Yes, I'm free. At what time?

A las seis y media.

At 6:30.

Perfecto. Nos vemos entonces.

Perfect. See you then.
