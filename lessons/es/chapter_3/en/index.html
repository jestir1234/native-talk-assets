Counting and Quantity Expressions

To ask "how many?" in Spanish, you use ¿Cuántos? 🔊 (for masculine nouns) or ¿Cuántas? 🔊 (for feminine nouns).

To say "there is" or "there are", use Hay 🔊.

🔊
¿Cuántos libros hay?
How many books are there?
🔊
Hay cinco libros.
There are five books.
🔊
Hay una manzana.
There is one apple.
Cultural Note: In Spanish, the number "one" (uno) changes form to agree with the gender of the noun. It's uno 🔊 for masculine nouns (e.g., un libro - one book) and una 🔊 for feminine nouns (e.g., una casa - one house). Numbers from "dos" (2) to "veinte" (20) typically do not change for gender.

Age: How old are you?

Asking someone's age in Spanish uses the verb tener 🔊 (to have), not "to be". So, you literally ask "How many years do you have?"

🔊
¿Cuántos años tienes?
How many years do you have? (How old are you?)
🔊
Tengo dieciocho años.
I have eighteen years. (I am eighteen years old.)
🔊
Ella tiene doce años.
She has twelve years. (She is twelve years old.)

Phone Numbers and Addresses

When giving a phone number, you usually say the digits individually. Addresses are also typically given out digit by digit for numbers.

The phrase for "My phone number is..." is Mi número de teléfono es... 🔊

The phrase for "My address is..." is Mi dirección es... 🔊

🔊
Mi número de teléfono es 555-1234.
My phone number is five, five, five, one, two, three, four.
🔊
Mi dirección es Calle Primavera, #15.
My address is Spring Street, number fifteen.
Cultural Note: Addresses in Spanish-speaking countries often place the street name first, then the building number (e.g., "Calle Mayor, número 10" - Main Street, number 10). Phone numbers are typically given digit by digit, sometimes grouped in pairs or threes, with pauses.

Basic Math Vocabulary

Even basic math terms are useful for understanding prices, quantities, and more.

  • Plus: Más 🔊
    (mahs)
  • Minus: Menos 🔊
    (MEH-nohs)
  • Equals: Es / Son 🔊 / 🔊
    (ehs / sohn)

Use "es" for singular results (e.g., "uno más uno es dos"). Use "son" for plural results (e.g., "dos más dos son cuatro").

🔊
1 + 1 = 2
Uno más uno es dos.
🔊
5 - 3 = 2
Cinco menos tres son dos.
🔊
10 + 5 = 15
Diez más cinco son quince.
