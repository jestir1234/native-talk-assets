Let's start with the core family members. Remember, in Spanish, nouns have gender (masculine or feminine) and number (singular or plural), and adjectives must agree!

Masculine Nouns (ending in -o, generally)

  • el padre (ehl PAH-dray) - the father
  • el hermano (ehl er-MAH-noh) - the brother
  • el hijo (ehl EE-hoh) - the son
  • el abuelo (ehl ah-BWEH-loh) - the grandfather

Feminine Nouns (ending in -a, generally)

  • la madre (lah MAH-dray) - the mother
  • la hermana (lah er-MAH-nah) - the sister
  • la hija (lah EE-hah) - the daughter
  • la abuela (lah ah-BWEH-lah) - the grandmother

Mi padre es alto.My father is tall.

Mi hermana es joven.My sister is young.

üôã‚Äç‚ôÄÔ∏è Possessive Adjectives: My, Your, His/Her/Its

To talk about whose family member it is, we use possessive adjectives. These words change depending on the noun they describe (its gender and number), not the owner!

Singular Possessive Adjectives

  • mi (mee) - my (for singular nouns, no gender change)

    Mi perro.My dog (masculine).

    Mi casa.My house (feminine).

  • tu (too) - your (informal, singular nouns)

    Tu libro.Your book.

  • su (soo) - his/her/its/your (formal)/their (singular nouns)

    This one is versatile! Context usually clarifies who "su" refers to. If needed, you can use "de √©l" (of him), "de ella" (of her), etc.

    Su madre.His/Her/Your (formal) mother.

Plural Possessive Adjectives

  • mis (mees) - my (for plural nouns)

    Mis padres.My parents (lit. my fathers, often used for both).

  • tus (toos) - your (informal, plural nouns)
  • sus (soos) - his/her/its/your (formal)/their (plural nouns)

And for "our" and "your" (plural, informal):

  • nuestro/a/os/as (NWEHS-troh/trah/tros/tras) - our (agrees in gender and number)

    Nuestro padre.Our father.

    Nuestras hermanas.Our sisters.

  • vuestro/a/os/as (BWES-troh/trah/tros/tras) - your (informal, plural - mainly used in Spain)

üí¨ This is my... / These are my...

Now let's put it all together to introduce family members! We use "Este/Esta es..." for singular and "Estos/Estas son..." for plural. Remember, "este/esta/estos/estas" must agree with the noun's gender and number.

  • Este es mi padre. - This is my father.
  • Esta es mi madre. - This is my mother.
  • Estos son mis hermanos. - These are my brothers (or siblings, if mixed gender).
  • Estas son mis hermanas. - These are my sisters.

Esta es su abuela.This is his/her/their grandmother.

Estos son nuestros hijos.These are our children/sons.
