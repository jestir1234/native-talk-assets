Chapter 2: Basic Greetings and Introductions

¡Hola! Welcome to Chapter 2! 👋

Ready to start chatting in Spanish? In this chapter, we'll learn essential greetings, introductions, and polite phrases that you'll use every day to make new friends and navigate conversations.

Saying Hello & Good Day ☀️🌙

Let's start with the basics! These are the first words you'll use when meeting someone or entering a place.

Hola [OH-lah] Hello / Hi
Buenos días [BWEH-nohs DEE-ahs] Good morning
Buenas tardes [BWEH-nahs TAR-dehs] Good afternoon / Good early evening
Buenas noches [BWEH-nahs NOH-chehs] Good evening / Good night

Cultural Note: Buenas tardes is used from midday until dusk. Buenas noches is used from dusk onwards, for both "good evening" and "good night" (when going to bed).

Farewells & Gratitude 🙏

Just as important as saying hello is knowing how to say goodbye and express thanks.

Adiós [ah-DYOHS] Goodbye
Gracias [GRAH-syahs] Thank you
De nada [deh NAH-dah] You're welcome

Introducing Yourself 👋

Time to tell people who you are! Here are two common ways:

Me llamo... [meh YAH-moh] My name is...

(Literally: "I call myself...")

Soy... [SOY] I am...

(Use "Soy" for professions, nationalities, or a quicker introduction)

Example:

  • Me llamo Ana. (My name is Ana.)
  • Soy David. (I am David.)

Making a Good Impression ✨

After introductions, it's polite to say "Nice to meet you."

Mucho gusto [MOO-choh GOOS-toh] Nice to meet you / Pleased to meet you

(Literally: "Much pleasure")

Example Dialogue:

  • Ana: Hola, me llamo Ana.
  • David: Mucho gusto, Ana. Soy David.

Being Polite 😊

Polite expressions are key to navigating any social situation.

Por favor [por fah-VOR] Please
Permiso / Con permiso [per-MEE-soh / kon per-MEE-soh] Excuse me (to pass by someone or if you need to briefly interrupt)
Lo siento [loh SYEN-toh] I'm sorry
¡Salud! [sah-LOOD] Bless you! (after a sneeze) / Cheers! (when toasting)
