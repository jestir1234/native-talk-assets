Chapter 7: Food and Drinks

🍽️ Get ready to tantalize your taste buds! In this chapter, we're diving into the delicious world of Korean food and drinks. You'll learn essential vocabulary, common dishes, and how to express whether something is delicious (or not!).

🍚 밥 (bap) - Rice / Meal

밥 (bap) is one of the most fundamental words in Korean cuisine, meaning both "cooked rice" and "a meal." Rice is such an integral part of Korean meals that "have you eaten 밥?" is a common greeting, similar to "how are you?"

밥 [bap]
아침밥 먹었어요?
Did you eat breakfast? (literally: Did you eat morning meal?)
저녁밥 먹어요.
I'm eating dinner. (literally: I'm eating evening meal.)
Cultural Note: In Korea, rice is not just a side dish; it's the center of almost every meal. It balances out the strong flavors of other dishes like kimchi and stews.

🌶️ 김치 (kimchi)

You can't talk about Korean food without mentioning 김치 (kimchi)! It's a staple side dish made of fermented vegetables, most commonly napa cabbage, seasoned with chili powder, garlic, and other spices.

김치 [kim-chi]
저는 김치 좋아해요.
I like kimchi.
Cultural Note: There are hundreds of varieties of kimchi! It's rich in probiotics and is considered incredibly healthy. Families often make large batches together during "Kimjang" season.

🥩 불고기 (bulgogi)

불고기 (bulgogi) is one of Korea's most famous dishes, beloved worldwide! It consists of thinly sliced beef (or sometimes pork) marinated in a savory-sweet sauce and then grilled.

불고기 [bul-go-gi]
불고기 맛있어요.
Bulgogi is delicious.

🥣 비빔밥 (bibimbap)

비빔밥 (bibimbap) literally means "mixed rice." It's a vibrant and healthy dish with a bowl of warm rice topped with various sautéed vegetables (나물 - namul), meat (often beef), a fried egg, and mixed with gochujang (Korean chili paste) before eating.

비빔밥 [bi-bim-bap]
비빔밥 하나 주세요.
Please give me one bibimbap.

☕ 커피 (keopi) & 🍵 차 (cha) - Coffee & Tea

Koreans love their beverages! Coffee shops are everywhere, and tea is also very popular.

커피 (keopi) - Coffee

커피 [keo-pi]
커피 한 잔 주세요.
Please give me one cup of coffee.

차 (cha) - Tea

차 [cha]
차 마셔요.
I drink tea.

😋 맛있다 / 맛없다 (mat-it-da / mat-eop-da) - Delicious / Not Delicious

These two words are essential for expressing your culinary opinions! They are adjectives.

맛있다 (mat-it-da) - Delicious

맛있다 [ma-sit-da]

Often used in its polite, conjugated form: 맛있어요 (ma-si-sseo-yo).

이 음식 맛있어요!
This food is delicious!

맛없다 (mat-eop-da) - Not Delicious

맛없다 [ma-deop-da]

Often used in its polite, conjugated form: 맛없어요 (ma-deop-sseo-yo).

이 김치 맛없어요.
This kimchi is not delicious.

🍽️ Ordering Food at Restaurants

Ready to order your meal in Korean? Here are some key phrases:

  • 주세요 (ju-se-yo) - Please give me / I'll have... (attach to the item)
  • 이거 (i-geo) - This (useful for pointing)
  • 하나 (ha-na) - One (native Korean number)
  • 몇 개 (myeot gae) - How many? (used with general counter '개')
  • 메뉴 주세요. (me-nyu ju-se-yo) - Menu, please.
  • 물 주세요. (mul ju-se-yo) - Water, please.

Putting it together:

김치 하나 주세요.
Please give me one kimchi. / I'll have one kimchi.
불고기 두 개 주세요.
Please give me two bulgogi. (Use '두 개' for two general items)
비빔밥 있어요?
Do you have bibimbap? (Literally: Is bibimbap existing?)
Cultural Note: 반찬 (banchan) When you eat at a Korean restaurant, you'll almost always be served an array of small side dishes called 반찬 (banchan), like kimchi, pickled radishes, and various vegetables. These are usually refillable for free! It's common to share food and side dishes with everyone at the table.

✨ Chapter Summary

You've just learned some fantastic vocabulary and phrases related to food and drinks in Korean! Let's recap what we covered:

  • 밥 - Rice / Meal
  • 김치 - Kimchi (staple fermented side dish)
  • 불고기 - Bulgogi (marinated grilled beef)
  • 비빔밥 - Bibimbap (mixed rice with vegetables)
  • 커피 - Coffee
  • 차 - Tea
  • 맛있다 / 맛있어요 - Delicious / It's delicious
  • 맛없다 / 맛없어요 - Not delicious / It's not delicious
  • [Item] 주세요. - Please give me [Item]. / I'll have [Item].
  • 메뉴 주세요. - Menu, please.
  • 물 주세요. - Water, please.

Practice these new words and phrases often, especially when you encounter Korean food! Enjoy your culinary adventures! 🍜
