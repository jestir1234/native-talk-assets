Chapter 2: Basic Greetings and Introductions

👋 Saying Hello & Goodbye: 안녕하세요

Let's kick off your Korean journey with the most essential phrase! 안녕하세요 (Annyeonghaseyo) is your versatile go-to for both "Hello" and "Goodbye" in many situations.

안녕하세요 🔊
Hello / Good morning / Good afternoon / Good evening / Goodbye

Pronunciation: Ahn-nyung-ha-say-yo

This phrase is polite and commonly used with people you don't know well, elders, or in formal settings. It literally means "Are you at peace?" or "Are you well?".

Cultural Note: Politeness Levels
Korean language has different politeness levels. The ending -요 (-yo), as seen in 안녕하세요, makes a phrase polite without being overly formal. It's perfect for most everyday interactions.

👋 Saying Goodbye (More Specifically): 안녕히 가세요 / 안녕히 계세요

While 안녕하세요 can work for goodbye, Koreans also use specific phrases depending on whether *you* are leaving or the *other person* is leaving. This shows more nuanced politeness.

1. When someone is leaving you (You are staying): 안녕히 가세요

Use 안녕히 가세요 (Annyeonghi gaseyo) when you are staying, and the other person is going. It literally means "Go peacefully."

안녕히 가세요 🔊
Goodbye (to someone leaving) / Please go in peace

Pronunciation: Ahn-nyung-hee gah-say-yo

2. When you are leaving someone (The other person is staying): 안녕히 계세요

Use 안녕히 계세요 (Annyeonghi gyeseyo) when you are leaving, and the other person is staying. It literally means "Stay peacefully."

안녕히 계세요 🔊
Goodbye (to someone staying) / Please stay in peace

Pronunciation: Ahn-nyung-hee geh-say-yo

🤝 Nice to Meet You: 만나서 반갑습니다

After a polite 안녕하세요, it's common to express that you're happy to meet someone.

만나서 반갑습니다 🔊
Nice to meet you

Pronunciation: Mahn-na-suh bahn-gahp-seup-nee-da

This phrase is quite polite and formal, suitable for first impressions. The -ㅂ니다/습니다 (-pnida/seupnida) ending is a more formal politeness level often used in presentations, news, or very formal settings, but it's perfectly natural and common for this particular greeting.

🗣️ Introducing Yourself: 저는 [이름]입니다

Time to tell people who you are! This is a very straightforward and polite way to introduce yourself.

저는 [이름]입니다 🔊
My name is [Name]

Pronunciation: Juh-neun [your name] im-nee-da

  • 저는 (jeo-neun): "I" (polite form) + topic marker.
  • [이름] ([i-reum]): Insert your name here.
  • 입니다 (im-nida): "am / is / are" (formal polite ending, same level as 반갑습니다).

Example: If your name is Sarah, you would say:

저는 사라입니다 🔊
My name is Sarah.

Pronunciation: Juh-neun sah-rah im-nee-da

This structure is very common for stating simple facts about yourself or others in a polite way.

🙏 Basic Polite Expressions: Thank You & Sorry

While not strictly "greetings," these are essential for polite interactions and complement your basic introduction skills.

Thank You: 감사합니다

감사합니다 🔊
Thank you (formal polite)

Pronunciation: Gahm-sa-ham-nee-da

This is the most common and polite way to say "Thank you." You'll hear and use it constantly!

Sorry / Excuse Me: 죄송합니다 / 실례합니다

There are a few ways to say sorry or excuse me. Here are two very useful ones:

죄송합니다 🔊
I am sorry / My apologies (formal polite)

Pronunciation: Jweh-song-ham-nee-da

Use 죄송합니다 when you want to sincerely apologize for something you did or when you feel bad for bothering someone.

실례합니다 🔊
Excuse me / Pardon me (formal polite - for interrupting or passing by)

Pronunciation: Shil-lye-ham-nee-da

Use 실례합니다 when you need to get someone's attention (like a waiter), pass through a crowded area, or when entering someone's space without permission (like opening a door to a private room).

Cultural Note: Bowing & Eye Contact
When greeting, especially with 안녕하세요 or 만나서 반갑습니다, a slight bow is very common and shows respect. The depth of the bow often corresponds to the level of respect you wish to show (deeper for elders or superiors). Direct eye contact can sometimes be considered less respectful when addressing elders or superiors, so it's common to slightly avert your gaze, especially during a bow.

✨ Chapter 2 Summary: Your First Steps!

  • 💡 안녕하세요 (Annyeonghaseyo): Your all-purpose "Hello" and "Goodbye" (polite).
  • 💡 안녕히 가세요 (Annyeonghi gaseyo): "Goodbye" when the other person is leaving (you are staying).
  • 💡 안녕히 계세요 (Annyeonghi gyeseyo): "Goodbye" when you are leaving (the other person is staying).
  • 💡 만나서 반갑습니다 (Mannaseo bangapseumnida): "Nice to meet you" (polite/formal).
  • 💡 저는 [이름]입니다 (Jeo-neun [name] imnida): "My name is [Name]" (polite/formal).
  • 💡 감사합니다 (Gamsahamnida): "Thank you" (polite/formal).
  • 💡 죄송합니다 (Joesonghamnida): "I am sorry / My apologies" (polite/formal).
  • 💡 실례합니다 (Sillyehamnida): "Excuse me / Pardon me" (polite/formal).
  • 💡 Korean politeness is key! Pay attention to endings like -요 and -ㅂ니다/습니다.
  • 💡 Don't forget a slight bow often accompanies greetings!
