Chapter 6: Daily Activities and Routines

Hey there, language learners! 👋 In this chapter, we're diving into the everyday actions that make up our day. From waking up to sleeping, you'll learn essential Korean verbs and time expressions to talk about your routine!

Let's get started and make your Korean conversations about daily life super smooth!

Essential Daily Verbs

These are core verbs you'll use constantly to describe what you do throughout the day. Remember, Korean verbs often end with -다 in their dictionary form.

1. 일어나다 (to wake up / to get up)

일어나다 [ee-luh-nah-dah]

This verb means 'to wake up' or 'to get up'. Use it when you're talking about starting your day!

저는 아침에 일찍 일어나요.

I wake up early in the morning.

몇 시에 일어나세요?

What time do you wake up?

2. 먹다 (to eat)

먹다 [muhk-dah]

One of the most important verbs! 먹다 means 'to eat'. You'll use this several times a day!

저는 아침을 먹어요.

I eat breakfast.

점심에 뭘 먹고 싶어요?

What do you want to eat for lunch?

3. 마시다 (to drink)

마시다 [mah-shee-dah]

Just like 'eat', 'drink' is essential. 마시다 is your go-to verb for beverages.

저는 물을 많이 마셔요.

I drink a lot of water.

커피 마실래요?

Do you want to drink coffee?

4. 공부하다 (to study)

공부하다 [gong-boo-hah-dah]

If you're learning Korean, you're definitely doing this! 공부하다 means 'to study'.

저는 한국어를 공부해요.

I study Korean.

매일 공부해야 해요.

I have to study every day.

5. 일하다 (to work)

일하다 [eel-hah-dah]

For your job or task, use 일하다, which means 'to work'.

저는 회사에서 일해요.

I work at a company.

오늘은 안 일해요.

I don't work today.

6. 자다 (to sleep)

자다 [jah-dah]

At the end of a long day, you 자다! This verb means 'to sleep'.

저는 밤에 늦게 자요.

I sleep late at night.

잘 자요!

Sleep well! (Good night!)

Time Expressions: Your Day in Korean ☀️

Now that you know some verbs, let's learn how to specify *when* these actions happen!

  • 아침 [ah-chim]: Morning / Breakfast

    Often used for "breakfast" when combined with "eat" (아침을 먹다).

    아침에 운동해요.

    I exercise in the morning.

  • 점심 [jum-shim]: Lunch / Noon

    Usually refers to the midday meal.

    점심은 1시에 먹어요.

    I eat lunch at 1 o'clock.

  • 저녁 [juh-nyuk]: Evening / Dinner

    Covers both the evening time and the dinner meal.

    저녁에 친구를 만나요.

    I meet a friend in the evening.

  • 밤 [bahm]: Night

    From late evening until morning. Don't confuse with 저녁 (early evening).

    밤에 책을 읽어요.

    I read a book at night.

Cultural Insight: Meal Names! In Korean, the words for 'breakfast' (아침), 'lunch' (점심), and 'dinner' (저녁) are the same as the words for 'morning', 'noon', and 'evening' respectively! So, to say "I eat breakfast", you say 아침을 먹어요 (literally "I eat morning").
