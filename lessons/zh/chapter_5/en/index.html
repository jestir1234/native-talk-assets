Chapter 5: Family and Relationships

👋 Welcome to Your Family!

In this chapter, you'll learn how to talk about your family members in Chinese. Family is super important in Chinese culture, so knowing these terms will help you connect with people and show respect. Let's get started!

👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Family Members

Let's learn the basic vocabulary for immediate family members. Pay close attention to the tones and practice saying them aloud!

Father

爸爸 bàba father / dad 🔊

Notice that 爸爸 (bàba) uses the same character twice. This is common for many family terms and makes them sound affectionate!

Mother

妈妈 māma mother / mom 🔊

Older Brother

哥哥 gēge older brother 🔊

Older Sister

姐姐 jiějie older sister 🔊

Younger Brother

弟弟 dìdi younger brother 🔊

Younger Sister

妹妹 mèimei younger sister 🔊

🏷️ Possessive Pronouns: My, Your, His/Her

To say "my," "your," or "his/her," we use the particle 的 (de) after the pronoun.

The Particle 的 (de)

的 (de) is a possessive particle. It's like adding an apostrophe 's' or "of" in English. It connects the possessor to the thing being possessed.

的 de possessive particle ('s, of) 🔊

My / 我的 (wǒ de)

我的 wǒ de my / mine 🔊

Example:

我的爸爸 wǒ de bàba 🔊
my father

Your / 你的 (nǐ de)

你的 nǐ de your / yours 🔊

Example:

你的妈妈 nǐ de māma 🔊
your mother

His / Her / 他的 (tā de) / 她的 (tā de)

Chinese has different characters for "he" (他 tā) and "she" (她 tā), but they sound exactly the same! The possessive form also sounds the same.

他的 tā de his 🔊
她的 tā de her / hers 🔊

Example:

她的弟弟 tā de dìdi 🔊
her younger brother

Their / 他们的 (tāmen de)

To say "they" or "their," we add 们 (men) to 他 (tā). This makes it plural.

他们的 tāmen de their / theirs 🔊

Example:

他们的哥哥 tāmen de gēge 🔊
their older brother

✨ "This is my..." / "These are my..."

To introduce someone, you can use the structure: 这 (zhè) 是 (shì) [possessive pronoun] [family member].

This is... / 这 是 (zhè shì)

这 zhè this 🔊
是 shì is / am / are (to be) 🔊

Example Sentences:

这是我的爸爸。 Zhè shì wǒ de bàba. 🔊
This is my father.
这是她的姐姐。 Zhè shì tā de jiějie. 🔊
This is her older sister.

Note on "These are": In Chinese, you often use "这 是" (zhè shì) for both "this is" and "these are" when referring to people. The context usually makes it clear, or you might refer to them individually.

🗣️ Talking About Family Members

Now let's put it all together to ask and answer simple questions about family!

Is this your...? / 这 是 你的... 吗？

To ask a yes/no question, simply add 吗 (ma) at the end of a statement.

吗 ma question particle (yes/no) 🔊

Example Question:

这是你的妹妹吗？ Zhè shì nǐ de mèimei ma? 🔊
Is this your younger sister?

Answering Yes:

是，这是我的妹妹。 Shì, zhè shì wǒ de mèimei. 🔊
Yes, this is my younger sister.

You can also simply say: 是 (Shì) - Yes, it is.

Answering No:

不是，这不是我的妹妹。 Bú shì, zhè bú shì wǒ de mèimei. 🔊
No, this is not my younger sister.

You can also simply say: 不是 (Bú shì) - No, it's not.

Who is this? / 这 是 谁？

To ask "who," we use 谁 (shéi).

谁 shéi who 🔊

Example Question:

这是谁？ Zhè shì shéi? 🔊
Who is this?

Example Answer:

这是我的妈妈。 Zhè shì wǒ de māma. 🔊
This is my mother.

Cultural Insight: Respect for Elders
In Chinese culture, family is the cornerstone of society, and respect for elders (尊重长辈 zūnzhòng zhǎngbèi) is a core value. Using the correct terms for older and younger siblings, and distinguishing between paternal and maternal grandparents, shows your understanding and respect for these cultural nuances. When greeting older family members, a slight bow or a respectful tone is often appreciated.

🎉 Chapter 5 Summary

Great job! You've learned a lot about talking about family in Chinese. Here's a quick recap:

  • Family Members: 爸爸 (bàba), 妈妈 (māma), 哥哥 (gēge), 姐姐 (jiějie), 弟弟 (dìdi), 妹妹 (mèimei), 爷爷 (yéye), 奶奶 (nǎinai), 外公 (wàigōng), 外婆 (wàipó), 儿子 (érzi), 女儿 (nǚ'ér), 孩子 (háizi), 家人 (jiārén).
  • Possessive Pronoun: Use 的 (de) after a pronoun to show possession (e.g., 我的 wǒ de - my).
  • Introducing Family: Use 这 是 (zhè shì) + [possessive pronoun] + [family member] to say "This is my...".
  • Asking Questions: Add 吗 (ma) at the end for yes/no questions, or use 谁 (shéi) for "who."
  • Answering: Use 是 (shì) for "yes" and 不是 (bú shì) for "no."

Keep practicing these terms and phrases. Talking about family is a fantastic way to connect with Chinese speakers!
