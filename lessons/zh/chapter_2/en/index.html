Chapter 2: Basic Greetings and Introductions

Welcome to Chapter 2! In this lesson, you'll learn essential Chinese phrases for saying hello, goodbye, thank you, and how to introduce yourself. Mastering these will give you a great start to interacting in Chinese!

👋 Greeting Someone

Let's start with the most common way to say hello in Chinese!

Hello: 你好 (nǐ hǎo)

你好 (nǐ hǎo)

This literally means "you good" and is the standard, polite way to say "hello" to one person. It's versatile and can be used in almost any situation!

Time-Specific Greetings

Just like in English, you can also use greetings specific to the time of day.

  • 早上好 (zǎo shang hǎo)

    Good morning!

  • 下午好 (xià wǔ hǎo)

    Good afternoon!

  • 晚上好 (wǎn shang hǎo)

    Good evening! (Can also be used as 'Good night' in some contexts)

Cultural Tip: While time-specific greetings are common, "你好 (nǐ hǎo)" is often used as an all-purpose greeting. In very informal settings or with close friends, Chinese people might just say someone's name, or skip a formal greeting altogether!

👋 Saying Goodbye, Thank You, and You're Welcome

These are super important for polite interactions!

Goodbye: 再见 (zài jiàn)

再见 (zài jiàn)

This literally means "again see" or "see you again". It's the standard way to say goodbye.

Thank you: 谢谢 (xiè xie)

谢谢 (xiè xie)

You'll hear this one all the time! The second character is often pronounced very lightly (neutral tone).

You're Welcome: 不客气 (bù kè qi) / 不用谢 (bú yòng xiè)

不客气 (bù kè qi)

"You're welcome" or "no need to be polite." This is very common.

不用谢 (bú yòng xiè)

Literally "no need thank you." Also commonly used, especially among friends or family.

Cultural Tip: While saying "谢谢" is good, sometimes Chinese people (especially family or close friends) might find it a bit too formal for small favors, implying a distance. However, it's always safe to use with strangers or in formal situations.

🗣️ Introducing Yourself

Time to tell people who you are!

My name is... / I'm...

There are two main ways to introduce your name:

  • 我叫 [Your Name] (wǒ jiào [Your Name])

    This means "I am called [Your Name]". It's the most common and natural way.

    Example: 我叫 大卫 (wǒ jiào Dàwèi)
    "My name is David."

  • 我是 [Your Name] (wǒ shì [Your Name])

    This means "I am [Your Name]". It's also acceptable, especially if you're stating your identity (e.g., "I am a student"). For simply introducing your name, 我叫 (wǒ jiào) is often preferred.

Nice to meet you: 很高兴认识你 (hěn gāo xìng rèn shi nǐ)

很高兴认识你 (hěn gāo xìng rèn shi nǐ)

This phrase means "Very happy to know you." It's a standard polite expression after an introduction.

Pronunciation Tip: The 'h' in Pinyin (like 'hǎo' or 'hěn') is a strong, aspirated 'h' sound, similar to the 'h' in "house" in English. Make sure to breathe out!

💖 Basic Polite Expressions

These phrases are crucial for showing respect and politeness.

  • 请 (qǐng)

    Please / May I ask...

    Use it when asking for something, like 请问 (qǐng wèn - "May I ask?").

  • 对不起 (duì bu qǐ)

    Sorry / I apologize

    Used for more serious apologies.

  • 不好意思 (bù hǎo yì si)

    Excuse me / I'm sorry (for a minor inconvenience)

    Often used for small things like bumping into someone, or getting someone's attention. More casual than 对不起.

Summary: Your First Steps in Chinese!

You've learned some fundamental phrases. Keep practicing to make them natural!

  • Greetings: 你好 (nǐ hǎo) for "hello", and time-specific greetings like 早上好 (zǎo shang hǎo).
  • Farewells: 再见 (zài jiàn) is the standard "goodbye".
  • Politeness: 谢谢 (xiè xie) for "thank you", and 不客气 (bù kè qi) or 不用谢 (bú yòng xiè) for "you're welcome".
  • Introductions: Use 我叫... (wǒ jiào...) to say "My name is...".
  • Meeting new people: Say 很高兴认识你 (hěn gāo xìng rèn shi nǐ) for "nice to meet you".
  • Other polite phrases: 请 (qǐng) for "please", 对不起 (duì bu qǐ) for "sorry", and 不好意思 (bù hǎo yì si) for "excuse me".

Great job! You now have the building blocks for basic conversations in Chinese. Keep reviewing these phrases!
